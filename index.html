About me

I’m a data analyst with a growing focus on data engineering, with a strong foundation in analytics, data pipelines, and business problem-solving. I enjoy working with messy, real-world data and transforming it into reliable, well-structured insights that support better decision-making.

I recently completed my Master’s in Business Analytics at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, where I worked extensively with large datasets using SQL, Python, and data visualization tools to solve operational and strategic problems. My academic work and projects focused on predictive modeling, supply chain analytics, cloud data platforms, advanced SQL, and building end-to-end analytical workflows.

Before my master’s program, I worked as a Data Analyst at a family-run retail business in Dubai, where I led the digitization of years of analog sales data, built dashboards for stakeholders, and improved profitability through data-driven inventory and pricing decisions. This experience gave me strong ownership, business context, and hands-on exposure to the full analytics lifecycle.

Outside of work and academics, sports have been a massive part of my life and continue to shape my interest in data analytics. I’m particularly drawn to how data is used in sports to analyze performance, optimize strategy, and tell compelling stories through numbers — an area where my analytical skills and personal passion naturally intersect.
